# Organizing a Legal Discussion | 组织法律论述

## 1 Introduction | 引言

The organization of your writing will determine whether or not a reader will understand and be persuaded by your argument. Brilliant rhetoric will only carry you so far—if your piece does not follow a clear structure, many of your points will be lost or misunderstood. As a result, it is crucial that your writing follow a clear organizational format that will be intelligible to your reader.

> 你文章的组织结构将决定读者能否理解并被你的论点说服。出色的修辞只能起一定作用——如果你的文章没有遵循清晰的结构，你的许多观点就会被遗漏或误解。因此，你的写作必须遵循一个读者能看懂的清晰组织格式。

Most legal writing requires the writer to analyze a set of facts using legal rules gleaned from a myriad of sources, including cases, statutes, and secondary materials. Unlike the non-legal writing you’ve done in college and at work, legal writing has its own specific structure that lawyers everywhere use in one form or another—and which they expect to see in your written work.

> 大多数法律写作要求作者使用从案例、法规和二手资料等大量来源中收集的法律规则来分析一组事实。与你在大学和工作中做的非法律写作不同，法律写作有其特定的结构，各地的律师都在以某种形式使用它，并且他们也期望在你的书面作品中看到它。

Whether they call it IRAC (Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion), CRAC (Conclusion, Rule, Application, Conclusion), or CREAC (Conclusion, Rule, Explanation, Application, Conclusion), all lawyers write in the same way: by laying out the issue to be discussed, the legal rule relevant to the issue, the analysis of the pertinent facts based on that rule, and the overall conclusion reached. Although this may sound daunting at first, it will quickly become second nature. Below is a primer on how to structure a legal argument using IRAC. CRAC and CREAC are incredibly similar to IRAC, and the same principles apply.

> 无论他们称之为 IRAC（争议点、规则、应用、结论），CRAC（结论、规则、应用、结论），还是 CREAC（结论、规则、解释、应用、结论），所有律师的写作方式都是相同的：列出要讨论的争议点，与该争议点相关的法律规则，基于该规则对相关事实的分析，以及得出的总体结论。虽然这乍听起来可能令人生畏，但它很快就会成为你的第二天性。以下是关于如何使用 IRAC 构建法律论述的入门指南。CRAC 和 CREAC 与 IRAC 非常相似，并且适用相同的原则。

---

## 2 Where do I use IRAC? | 哪里需要使用 IRAC？

IRAC is used after your facts section, in the ‘discussion’ section or your memo, or the ‘argument’ section of your brief. Each discrete legal topic will have its own IRAC structure, under a separate sub-heading. For example, an affirmative defense and a necessary element of a claim would each receive their own complete, independent IRAC discussions.

> IRAC 用在事实陈述部分之后，即备忘录（memo）的“讨论（discussion）”部分，或诉状（brief）的“辩论（argument）”部分。每一个独立的法律主题都将在一个单独的副标题下拥有自己的 IRAC 结构。例如，一个积极抗辩（affirmative defense）和一个主张的必要构成要件（necessary element），都将分别获得各自完整的、独立的 IRAC 论述。

---

## 3 How do I use IRAC? | 如何使用 IRAC？

With practice, it will feel entirely natural to organize your legal discussion following the IRAC form. In the meantime, below is a basic outline of the IRAC format and its best uses.

> 通过练习，你会觉得按照 IRAC 格式组织法律讨论是完全自然的。在此之前，以下是 IRAC 格式的基本大纲及其最佳用途。

### 3.1 Issue | 争议点

State the issue in the first paragraph at the beginning of the sub-section: what is the legal question you will need to analyze? Why do you need to analyze this issue? This first section should give your reader an understanding of what you intend to discuss and why you must discuss it.

> 在子部分开头的首段陈述争议点：你需要分析的法律问题是什么？你为什么需要分析这个问题？这第一部分应该让读者了解你打算讨论什么以及为什么要讨论它。

In a **memo**, you should be neutral in your statement of the facts while also predicting how the judge will rule on the issue.

> 在**备忘录 (memo)** 中，你在陈述事实时应保持中立，同时预测法官将如何对该问题作出裁决。

- **Best (最佳):** State the relevant issue in a way that reveals your conclusion. (以能揭示你结论的方式陈述相关的争议点。)
    
    - _Example:_ The Court will likely rule that Officer used unconstitutionally excessive force under the Graham test as applied to the facts of this case.
        
    - _示例:_ 法院可能会裁定，根据适用于本案事实的格雷厄姆（Graham）测试标准，警官使用了违宪的过度武力。
        
- **Good (良好):** State the relevant issue in a neutral fashion. (以中立的方式陈述相关的争议点。)
    
    - _Example:_ The judge must then decide whether the balancing test in Graham warrants a finding of excessive force.
        
    - _示例:_ 然后，法官必须决定格雷厄姆案中的平衡测试是否支持过度武力的认定。
        
- **Not Good (不佳):** State the relevant issue as a question. (将相关争议点陈述为一个问句。)
    
    - _Example:_ Did the Officer use excessive force under the Graham test? _(Note that using the question format is stylistically disfavored in the legal profession.)_
        
    - _示例:_ 根据格雷厄姆测试，警官是否使用了过度武力？_（注：在法律界，使用问句格式在文体上是不受青睐的。）_
        

In a **brief**, you should be more opinionated and assert how your client would like the issue to be resolved.

> 在**诉状 (brief)** 中，你应该更具主张性，并断言你的客户希望该问题如何得到解决。

- **Best (最佳):** Assert that the relevant issue should come out in your client’s favor and (briefly) explain why. (断言相关争议点的结果应有利于你的客户，并简要解释原因。)
    
    - _Example:_ The balancing test in Graham warrants a finding of excessive force because Officer responded to an unthreatening suspect with a serious intrusion into his Fourth Amendment rights.
        
    - _示例:_ 格雷厄姆案中的平衡测试支持过度武力的认定，因为警官对一个没有威胁的嫌疑人做出了反应，严重侵犯了其第四修正案权利。
        
- **Good (良好):** Assert that the relevant issue should come out in your client’s favor. (断言相关争议点的结果应有利于你的客户。)
    
    - _Example:_ The court should find that the officer used excessive force under the balancing test in Graham.
        
    - _示例:_ 法院应认定，根据格雷厄姆案中的平衡测试，该警官使用了过度武力。
        
- **Not Good (不佳):** State the relevant issue in a neutral fashion. (以中立的方式陈述相关争议点。)
    
    - _Example:_ The court will need to employ the balancing test in Graham to decide whether the officer used excessive force.
        
    - _示例:_ 法院将需要采用格雷厄姆案中的平衡测试来决定该警官是否使用了过度武力。
        

---

### 3.2 Rule/Explanation | 规则与解释

After you lay out the issue, you will need to establish the governing legal rule that the court will employ to resolve that issue. Your rule section should resemble a funnel: set out the broadest principles first, with the smaller, secondary components, or exceptions to the rule following afterwards. Generally, you will be able to naturally create a funnel by discussing authorities in order from most important to least important. State holdings of cases briefly, and only include relevant facts and conclusions. Depending on the nature of your case, you may also wish to include a paragraph discussing particularly relevant precedent in order to establish how the rule works in practice.

> 在列出争议点之后，你需要确立法院将用来解决该争议点的适用法律规则。你的规则部分应该像一个漏斗：首先列出最宽泛的原则，随后是较小的次要组成部分，或该规则的例外情况。通常，你可以通过按从最重要到最不重要的顺序讨论权威来源来自然地创建一个漏斗。简要陈述案件的判决（holdings），且仅包含相关事实和结论。根据你案件的性质，你可能还希望包含一段讨论特别相关的先例，以确立该规则在实践中是如何运作的。

- **Order of Authorities (权威来源顺序):** Constitution, statutes, regulations, Supreme Court cases, appellate court cases, trial court cases, and lastly, secondary sources. (宪法、法规、规章、最高法院判例、上诉法院判例、初审法院判例，最后是二手资料。)
    
- **General to specific** (一般法则  具体规定)
    
- **Baseline rule to exceptions** (基本规则  例外情况)
    
- **Tip (提示):** For concise use of legal sources, use ellipses (Bluebook R. 5.3), and minimize use of block quotations. (为了简明扼要地使用法律来源，请使用省略号【蓝皮书规则 5.3】，并尽量减少整段引用的使用。)
    
- **Explain the whole rule; don’t just give a one-liner.** (解释整个规则；不要只给出一句单行语。)
    

> **Example (示例 1):** It is well established that “the use of force is contrary to the Fourth Amendment if it is excessive under objective standards of reasonableness.” _Saucier v. Katz_... The reasonableness of the application of force applied by a police officer depends on a balancing of the force applied and the circumstances confronted by the officer. “A claim that excessive force was used in the course of a seizure is subject to an objective test of reasonableness under the totality of the circumstances of each case, including the severity of the crime at issue, whether the suspect posed an immediate threat to the safety of others, and whether he is actively resisting arrest.” _Sullivan v. Gagnier_... Under the law, police are not permitted to use any degree of force in all instances—in some circumstances, no use of force is reasonable because none is required. _Bauer v. Norris_... The Second Circuit has held that the degree of injury is not determinative of an excessive force claim; even an injury that is not permanent or severe can suffice. _Robinson v. Via_...
> 
> **中文翻译:** 众所周知，“如果根据客观合理性标准，武力的使用是过度的，那么它就违反了第四修正案。” [引用略] 警官施加武力的合理性取决于所施加武力与警官面临的情况之间的平衡。“在扣押过程中使用过度武力的主张，需要根据每个案件的整体情况（包括所涉犯罪的严重程度、嫌疑人是否对他人安全构成直接威胁，以及他是否在积极拒捕）进行客观合理性测试。” [引用略] 根据法律，警察在任何情况下都不被允许任意使用武力——在某些情况下，不使用任何武力才是合理的，因为根本不需要使用武力。 [引用略] 第二巡回法院裁定，伤害程度并非过度武力主张的决定性因素；即使是不永久或不严重的伤害也足以构成过度武力。 [引用略]

> **Example (示例 2):** When applying the balancing test in Graham, the court has held that the there is little governmental interest in arresting a suspect for a minor offense. _See Jones v. Parmley_... _Thomas v. Roach_... Therefore, a suspect’s alleged crime must be sufficiently serious to warrant use of painful force, such as a taser, under a Graham analysis. _Tennessee v. Garner_... Given that the threat posed by the suspect is “the most important single element” of the Graham analysis, _Chew v. Gates_..., any arrest in which the suspect poses no threat and is only wanted for a minor infraction likely does not give rise to a significant governmental interest.
> 
> **中文翻译:** 在应用格雷厄姆（Graham）案中的平衡测试时，法院认为，因轻微违法行为逮捕嫌疑人的政府利益微乎其微。 [引用略] 因此，在 Graham 分析框架下，嫌疑人涉嫌的犯罪必须足够严重，才值得使用令人痛苦的武力（如电击枪）。 [引用略] 鉴于嫌疑人构成的威胁是 Graham 分析中“最重要的单一要素” [引用略]，任何嫌疑人未构成威胁且仅因轻微违规被通缉的逮捕，通常都不会产生重大的政府利益。

---

### 3.3 Application | 应用

In this section, you will apply the rule to your facts, using the cases you’ve discussed in the rule section to draw analogies or distinctions. You should track the order and key phrases of the Rule section so that your reader can easily follow along. Don’t be afraid to repeat key terms and phrases—you will frequently need to do so to show that your case follows precedent. This section will be the bulk of your argument, and may run several paragraphs or pages long.

> 在这一部分，你需要将规则应用到事实中，使用你在“规则”部分讨论过的案例进行类比或区分。你应该遵循规则部分的顺序和关键短语，以便读者能轻松跟上。不要害怕重复关键术语和短语——你经常需要这样做来表明你的案件遵循了先例。这一部分将是你论证的主体，可能会长达数段或数页。

> **Example (示例):** In the instant matter, the officer’s use of force against Victim was objectively unreasonable because Victim committed only a minor offense and posed no threat to Officer. Officer arrested Victim for loitering under New York Penal Law § 240.35, which classifies the infraction as a violation – a lower grade than even a misdemeanor. This infraction is even less serious than the one at issue Thomas (verbal threats) and is equivalent to the minor ones in Jones (protest violation). Moreover, Victim posed so little threat to Officer that sanctioning taser use in this situation would run contrary to precedent and notions of justice. Victim did not approach Officer or manifest any intention to harm him. Much like in Tennessee v. Garner, 471 U.S. at 21, where substantial force was unreasonable because the fleeing suspect posed no threat to the officer, Victim was actually attempting to escape away from Officer.
> 
> **中文翻译:** 在本案中，警官对受害者使用武力在客观上是不合理的，因为受害者只犯了轻微违规行为，且对警官没有构成威胁。警官根据纽约刑法 § 240.35 以游荡罪逮捕了受害者，该法将此违规行为归类为违法（violation）——其等级甚至低于轻罪（misdemeanor）。这一违规行为比 _Thomas_ 案中的问题（言语威胁）还要轻微，等同于 _Jones_ 案中的轻微违规（抗议违规）。此外，受害者对警官构成的威胁微乎其微，如果在这种情况下批准使用电击枪，将有悖于先例和正义理念。受害者没有靠近警官，也没有表现出任何伤害他的意图。正如在 _Tennessee v. Garner_ 案中一样（由于逃跑的嫌疑人对警官没有构成威胁，因此使用实质性武力是不合理的），受害者在本案中实际上正试图逃离警官。

---

### 3.4 Conclusion | 结论

Here, all you will need is a sentence or two that concisely state the outcome of the issue, based on the Application of the Rule to the facts of the case.

> 在这里，你只需要一两句话，基于“规则”在案件事实上的“应用”结果，简明扼要地陈述该争议点的最终结论。

> **Example (示例):** Therefore, because Victim posed no threat to Officer and was only liable for a minor infraction, Officer’s use of force was excessive under Graham.
> 
> **中文翻译:** 因此，由于受害者对警官没有构成威胁，并且仅对轻微违法行为负责，根据 Graham 案的标准，警官使用的武力是过度的。

---

## 4 Putting it all together | 融会贯通

Fully synthesized, IRAC will allow you to move from the main problems in a case through the governing law, and to a final conclusion. Consider one final example. Your client is getting divorced in Connecticut. Her husband argues that she did not fairly and reasonably disclose her property, which Connecticut law requires, because her disclosure inaccurately stated her overall assets. In a memo, you might analyze this point like this:

> 将上述内容综合起来，IRAC 将使你能够从案件的主要问题平滑过渡到适用法律，并得出最终结论。考虑最后一个例子：你的客户正在康涅狄格州办理离婚。她的丈夫辩称，她没有按照康涅狄格州法律的要求“公平合理地”披露其财产，因为她的披露不准确地陈述了她的总资产。在备忘录中，你可以这样分析这一点：

**ISSUE, or Topic Sentence | 争议点或主题句**

> A court will not be convinced that my client’s financial disclosures are ‘incomplete.’ **中文:** 法院不会采信我方客户的财务披露“不完整”的说法。

**RULE | 规则**

> A “‘fair and reasonable’ disclosure refers to the nature, extent and accuracy of the information to be disclosed.” _Friezo v. Friezo_, 914 A.2d 533, 545 (Conn. 2007). Friezo notes that “a fair and reasonable financial disclosure requires each contracting party to provide the other with a general approximation of their income, assets and liabilities.” 914 A.2d at 550. **中文:** “‘公平合理’的披露是指要披露信息的性质、程度和准确性。” [引用略] _Friezo_ 案指出，“公平合理的财务披露要求立约各方向对方提供其收入、资产和负债的总体估算。”

**ANALYSIS: Interpret the Evidence | 分析：解释证据**

> In Friezo, the defendant provided “an accurate representation, in writing,” that “set forth a list of the defendant’s assets and liabilities, most of which were valued individually.” Id. at 551, 550. Here, my client provided a similarly detailed written valuation. Her husband’s claims that the schedules omit key information about the value of my client’s real estate holdings and miscalculate her total assets, undervaluing them by $1,000,000, are inaccurate. My client provided either statements of value or recent assessments of value for each of her properties holdings to her husband. While Schedule A inaccurately states my client’s total assets, this misstatement is a clerical error; each of her properties is accurately valued individually. **中文:** 在 _Friezo_ 案中，被告提供了一份“准确的书面说明”，该说明“列出了被告的资产和负债表，其中大部分是单独估值的。” [引用略] 在本案中，我方客户提供了一份同样详细的书面估值。她丈夫声称附表遗漏了关于我方客户房地产资产价值的关键信息，并且错误计算了她的总资产，将其低估了 1,000,000 美元，这些说法都是不准确的。我方客户向她丈夫提供了她每一项财产的价值声明或近期的价值评估。虽然附表 A 不准确地陈述了我方客户的总资产，但这只是笔误；她的每一项单独财产都得到了准确评估。

**CONCLUSION: Reconnect This Point to Your Thesis | 结论：将此观点与你的核心论点相联系**

> Since Connecticut requires only a “general approximation” of assets, a court will find my client’s disclosure to be fair and reasonable. **中文:** 由于康涅狄格州仅要求提供资产的“总体估算”，法院将认定我方客户的披露是公平合理的。

# 另一个版本

# Organizing a Legal Discussion: IRAC / CRAC / CREAC | 组织法律论述：IRAC / CRAC / CREAC

Legal writing should follow a clear structure; otherwise your points risk being lost or misunderstood. Most legal writing in the U.S. requires the writer to analyze a set of facts using legal rules gleaned from sources including cases, statutes, regulations, and secondary materials. In this context, legal writing has a very specific organizational structure that you may see described as IRAC (Issue, Rule, Application, Conclusion), CRAC (Conclusion, Rule, Application, Conclusion), or CREAC (Conclusion, Rule, Explanation, Application, Conclusion).

> 法律写作应遵循清晰的结构；否则你的观点就有被遗漏或误解的风险。美国大多数的法律写作要求作者使用从案例、法规、规章和二手资料等来源中收集的法律规则来分析一组事实。在这种背景下，法律写作具有非常具体的组织结构，你可能会看到它被描述为 IRAC（争议点、规则、应用、结论）、CRAC（结论、规则、应用、结论）或 CREAC（结论、规则、解释、应用、结论）。

While CRAC and CREAC are more often used in persuasive writing, the underlying structure for all three methods is the same: 1) clearly state the issue and/or conclusion, 2) set forth the relevant legal rule(s), 3) apply and analyze the pertinent facts based on the governing rule(s), and 4) state—or restate—the conclusion.

> 虽然 CRAC 和 CREAC 更常用于说服性写作（persuasive writing），但这三种方法的底层结构是相同的：1) 明确陈述争议点和/或结论，2) 阐明相关的法律规则，3) 基于适用的规则应用和分析相关事实，以及 4) 陈述（或重申）结论。

---

## 1 Placement of IRAC / CRAC / CREAC | 方法的适用位置

Use IRAC, CRAC, or CREAC to evaluate specific legal issues in the argument or discussion section of your brief, memo, or paper. Each discrete legal topic should have its own IRAC, CRAC, or CREAC structure, with subissues under separate subheadings. For example, in analyzing various affirmative defenses to a claim, each affirmative defense would receive its own complete, independent IRAC, CRAC, or CREAC section.

> 在诉状、备忘录或论文的辩论（argument）或讨论（discussion）部分，使用 IRAC、CRAC 或 CREAC 来评估特定的法律问题。每一个独立的法律主题都应有其自己的 IRAC、CRAC 或 CREAC 结构，并将子问题放在单独的副标题下。例如，在分析针对某项主张的各种积极抗辩（affirmative defenses）时，每一个积极抗辩都将获得其自身完整、独立的 IRAC、CRAC 或 CREAC 部分。

---

## 2 Elements of IRAC / CRAC / CREAC | 方法的基本要素

### 2.1 Issue and/or Conclusion | 争议点和/或结论

State the issue in the first paragraph at the beginning of the section or subsection. Clearly state the legal issue you will analyze and/or the conclusion. If you are using IRAC, state the issue inclusive of the likely conclusion, if possible. If you are using CRAC or CREAC, state the conclusion in a way that makes the issue clear.

> 在部分或子部分开头的首段陈述争议点。清楚地陈述你将分析的法律问题和/或结论。如果你使用的是 IRAC，请尽可能在陈述争议点时包含可能的结论。如果你使用的是 CRAC 或 CREAC，请以能让争议点清晰明了的方式陈述结论。

**In a memo (Objective Analysis) | 在备忘录中（客观分析）:**

- **Best (最佳):** A court will likely find that Re-Shoes is excluded from VARA since it constitutes advertising or promotional material.
    
    _(法院可能会认定 Re-Shoes 被排除在 VARA 的保护之外，因为它构成了广告或促销材料。)_
    
- **Generally Avoid (通常应避免):** Is Re-Shoes excluded from VARA?
    
    _(Re-Shoes 是否被排除在 VARA 的保护之外？)_
    

**In a brief (Persuasive Writing) | 在诉状或其他说服性写作中:**

- **Best (最佳):** Re-Shoes is excluded from VARA protection because it promotes the ideal of sustainability and qualifies as advertising or promotional material.

- **Generally Avoid (通常应避免):** The court will need to decide whether Re-Shoes is excluded from VARA protection.

### 2.2 Rule and Rule Explanation | 规则与规则解释

Next, describe and explain the legal rule(s) that govern the issue. Your rule section should resemble a funnel: set out the broadest principles first, with the smaller, secondary components or exceptions to the rule following afterwards. You can create a funnel by discussing authorities in order from most important to least important.

> 接下来，描述并解释管辖该问题的法律规则。你的规则部分应该像一个漏斗：首先列出最宽泛的原则，随后是较小的次要组成部分或规则的例外情况。你可以通过按从最重要到最不重要的顺序讨论权威来源（authorities）来创建一个漏斗。

State holdings of cases briefly, and only include relevant facts and conclusions. Your rule section should not simply list cases that apply. Rather, you should synthesize and explain the governing rule(s) you extracted from those cases. Depending on the nature of your case, you may also wish to include a paragraph discussing particularly relevant precedent in order to establish how the rule works in practice, or a definition of a relevant term or phrase.

> 简要陈述案件的判决（holdings），仅包含相关事实和结论。你的规则部分不应该仅仅列出适用的案例。相反，你应该综合并解释你从这些案例中提取的管辖规则。根据你案件的性质，你可能还希望包含一段专门讨论特别相关的先例，以确立该规则在实践中是如何运作的，或者提供相关术语/短语的定义。

**Keep in mind the following points | 请牢记以下要点：**

- **Order of Authorities:** Constitution, statutes, regulations, Supreme Court cases, appellate court cases, trial court cases, and, if necessary and applicable, secondary sources.
    
    _(**权威来源顺序：** 宪法、法规、规章、最高法院判例、上诉法院判例、初审法院判例，以及在必要且适用时使用二手资料。)_
    
- **General to Specific:** Outline general rules first, then more specific ones.
    
    _(**从一般到具体：** 首先概述一般规则，然后再概述更具体的规则。)_
    
- **Baseline to Exceptions:** Start with the baseline rule, then note any exceptions.
    
    _(**从基准到例外：** 从基本规则开始，然后指出任何例外情况。)_
    
- **Conciseness:** For concise use of legal sources, use ellipses (Bluebook R. 5.3), and minimize use of block quotations.
    
    _(**简明扼要：** 为了简明地引用法律来源，请使用省略号 [蓝皮书规则 5.3]，并尽量减少大段引用的使用。)_
    

**Example | 示例：**

> VARA covers only “works of visual art,” which exclude “advertising and promotional material.” 17 U.S.C. § 101 (2016). The statute does not define the terms “advertising” or “promotional.” Instead, courts have turned to the dictionary definitions of these terms. _See Martin_, 982 F. Supp. at 632; _Pollara_, 206 F. Supp. 2d at 337. In response to the lack of relevant authority, the _Martin_ court noted that the dictionary defines “advertise” as “to call public attention to especially by emphasizing desirable qualities so as to arouse a desire to buy or patronize.” _Martin_, 982 F. Supp. at 632 (quoting Webster’s Third Int’l Dictionary (1993)). Following suit, the Northern District of New York further defined “advertising” as “the act of attracting attention to a product or business,” and “promotion” as “advertising or other publicity.” _Pollara_, 206 F. Supp. 2d at 337 (citing American Heritage Dictionary).
> 
> **中文翻译：**
> 
> VARA 仅涵盖“视觉艺术作品”，该定义排除了“广告和促销材料”。17 U.S.C. § 101 (2016)。该法规未定义“广告”或“促销”一词。相反，法院求助于这些词语的字典定义。参见 _Martin_ 案；_Pollara_ 案。针对相关权威资料的缺乏，_Martin_ 案法院指出，字典将“广告”定义为“特别是通过强调可取的品质来引起公众注意，从而激起购买或光顾的欲望”。[引用略]。紧随其后，纽约北区法院进一步将“广告”定义为“吸引人们对产品或业务的注意力的行为”，将“促销”定义为“广告或其他宣传活动”。[引用略]。

### 2.3 Application | 应用

In the application section, apply the rule(s) to the facts, using the legal authority outlined in the rule section to draw analogies or distinctions. You should follow the order and use the same key phrases as in the rule section so your reader can easily follow along. Don’t be afraid to repeat key terms and phrases—you will frequently need to do so to show that your case follows precedent. The application section will usually be the bulk of your argument.

> 在应用部分，将规则应用到事实中，使用在“规则”部分概述的法律权威进行类比（analogies）或区分（distinctions）。你应该遵循与“规则”部分相同的顺序并使用相同的关键短语，以便读者能够轻松跟上。不要害怕重复关键术语和短语——你经常需要这样做来证明你的案件遵循了先例。“应用”部分通常是你论证的主体。

**Example | 示例：**

> Based on the dictionary definitions of promotion and advertising, a court should find that Ms. Sund’s work promotes the ideal of sustainability. The _Pollara_ court notes that even promotion of a non-commercial nature still excludes a work from VARA protection. 344 F.3d at 270. “Re-Shoes” does “call public attention to” ideas of sustainability “so as to arouse a desire to . . . patronize” environmental efforts, thus fitting into the _Martin_ definition of advertising. _Martin_, 982 F. Supp. at 632 (citing Webster’s Third Int’l Dictionary (1993)). The imagery of a tree, created by shoes that are themselves upcycled materials, clearly signals a message of environmental advocacy. The public’s reception of “Re-Shoes” elucidates the “objective and evident” purpose of “Re-Shoes”; media coverage of the sculpture notes its effect of raising awareness of environmental sustainability. _Pollara_, 344 F.3d at 269.
> 
> **中文翻译：**
> 
> 基于促销和广告的字典定义，法院应该认定 Sund 女士的作品宣传了可持续发展的理念。_Pollara_ 案法院指出，即使是非商业性质的促销活动也会将作品排除在 VARA 保护之外。[引用略]。“Re-Shoes”确实“引起了公众对”可持续发展理念的“注意”，“从而激起了……支持”环保工作的“欲望”，因此符合 _Martin_ 案对广告的定义。[引用略]。由本身即为升级回收材料的鞋子创造的树木意象，清晰地传递了倡导环保的信息。公众对“Re-Shoes”的接受度阐明了“Re-Shoes”的“客观和明显的”目的；媒体对该雕塑的报道指出了其提高环境可持续性意识的效果。[引用略]。
